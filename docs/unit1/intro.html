Introduction to Algebra

Learning Targets

  1. Interpret linear, quadratic, and exponential expressions in terms of a context by viewing one or more of their parts as a single entity.
  2. Choose and produce an equivalent form of an expression to reveal and explain properties of the quantity represented by the expression.
    1. Factor quadratic expressions with leading coefficients of one, and use the factored form to reveal the zeros of the function it defines.
    1. Use the vertex form of a quadratic expression to reveal the maximum or minimum value and the axis of symmetry of the function it defines; complete the square to find the vertex form of quadratics with a leading coefficient of one.
    1. Use the properties of exponents to transform expressions for exponential functions.
  • Example: Identify percent rate of change in functions such as \(y = (1.02)^{t}\), \(y = (0.97)^{t}\), \(y = (1.01)^{12t}\), \(y = (1.2)^{t/10}\), and classify them as representing exponential growth or decay.
